Waveform Analysis

Transient simulation waveforms showing signal progression through each stage of the power supply.

Four-stage waveform progression: AC input from transformer secondary, full-wave rectified output, capacitor-filtered DC with ripple, and final regulated 12V output.

Zoomed view of the ripple voltage before and after regulation. The 2200uF capacitor reduces ripple to ~533mV, and the LM7812 regulator eliminates remaining ripple to produce clean 12V DC.

Load regulation sweep showing output voltage stability across varying load resistance. The regulator maintains 12V for loads above approximately 20 ohms, demonstrating excellent regulation characteristics.

Filter capacitor frequency response (Bode magnitude plot). The 2200uF capacitor provides significant attenuation at the 120Hz ripple frequency (2x line frequency for full-wave rectification).

D
Design Methodology

Step-by-step approach used in the design and verification of this power supply.

1

Requirements Analysis

Defined output specifications: 12V DC, 120mA minimum load current, low ripple voltage, and line/load regulation targets.

2

Transformer Selection

Selected 120V/12Vrms step-down transformer providing 17V peak secondary voltage, sufficient headroom for bridge rectifier drops and regulator dropout.

3

Rectifier & Filter Design

Full-wave bridge rectifier (1N4007) with 2200uF electrolytic filter capacitor. Ripple calculated: V_ripple = I_load / (2 * f * C) = 533mV.

4

Regulator Integration

LM7812 linear regulator with 100uF output decoupling capacitor. Provides >60dB ripple rejection and thermal protection.

5

SPICE Simulation

Complete circuit simulated in NGSPICE with transient analysis (100ms), DC sweep for load regulation, and AC analysis for frequency response.

6

Verification & Validation

Simulation results cross-checked against analytical calculations. Output voltage, ripple, and regulation characteristics meet all design specifications.
